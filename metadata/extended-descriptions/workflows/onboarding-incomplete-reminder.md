### What this template solves

- Recovers users who sign up but abandon onboarding before completing setup.
- Drives activation by reminding users of the specific step they left incomplete.
- Uses a low-friction in-app nudge before escalating to email.
- Improves trial-to-activated conversion rates without aggressive sales tactics.

---

### When to use this template

Use this template when:

- Your product has a multi-step onboarding flow with measurable drop-off.
- Users create accounts but don't complete key setup steps (workspace, integration, invite).
- You want to re-engage users who logged in once but didn't finish configuring.
- Activation metrics show a gap between signups and meaningful product usage.

---

### How it works (step-by-step)

1. **Trigger.** User abandons onboarding with a specific incomplete step identified.
2. **In-app nudge.** Show a feed notification pointing to the remaining setup step.
3. **Wait 24 hours.** Give the user time to return on their own.
4. **Email reminder.** Send a friendly email with a direct link to continue setup.
5. **Cancel on completion.** Cancel the workflow when the user completes the step.

---

### Best practices

- **Name the specific step.** "Complete your workspace setup" beats "Finish onboarding."
- **Show progress.** Remind users how close they are: "You're 1 step away from being ready."
- **One CTA.** Link directly to the incomplete step, not the generic homepage.
- **Limit reminders.** One in-app nudge and one email is enough; don't nag daily.

---

### Common mistakes to avoid

- **Generic reminders.** "Come back to our app" doesn't tell users what to do next.
- **Reminding completed users.** Always cancel the workflow when onboarding finishes.
- **Too many steps in one email.** Focus on the single next action, not the entire checklist.

---

### FAQ

**When should I trigger this workflow?**  
 After a user has been inactive for a few hours post-signup, or when they explicitly skip a step.

**How many reminders should I send?**  
 Start with one in-app + one email. Add a second email only if data shows it improves conversion.

**Should I offer help?**  
 Yes. Include a link to docs, a demo, or support for users who seem stuck on a specific step.
