### What this template solves

- Creates positive reinforcement loop that encourages more referrals.
- Provides closure to referrers who wonder if their invites were received.
- Triggers reward/incentive fulfillment at the right moment.
- Builds viral growth momentum by celebrating successful connections.

---

### When to use this template

Use this template when:

- You have a referral program with rewards or recognition for successful invites.
- Your growth strategy depends on word-of-mouth and user advocacy.
- You want to strengthen social connections between referrer and referee.
- Gamification or social proof elements are part of your engagement strategy.
- Network effects make each new user more valuable to existing users.

---

### How it works (step-by-step)

1. **Trigger.** Referred user completes signup/activation via referral link.
2. **Verify referral.** Confirm valid referral relationship and eligibility.
3. **Notify referrer.** Send celebration notification to original referrer. Use a multi-channel strategy to escalate from in-app to out-of-app channels.
4. **Include reward details.** Communicate any earned benefits or status updates.
5. **Encourage next action.** Suggest connecting with new user or sending more invites.

---

### Best practices

- **Celebrate immediately.** Strike while enthusiasm is high. Delayed gratification reduces impact and future referral likelihood.
- **Make it personal.** Include referee's name/photo if available. "Your friend Sarah just joined\!" beats "Referral accepted."
- **Show tangible value.** If rewards exist, show progress clearly: "You've earned $10 credit\! Invite 2 more friends for bonus rewards."
- **Enable connection.** Include options for referrer to welcome, follow, or collaborate with their successful referral.

---

### Common mistakes to avoid

- **Generic success messages.** "Referral accepted" feels transactional. Make it celebratory and personal to drive emotional engagement.
- **Hiding the reward.** If incentives exist, state them clearly. Don't make users hunt for what they've earned.
- **Not closing the loop.** Both parties should know the connection succeeded. Consider notifying referee that their friend is excited they joined.
- **Overwhelming with next steps.** Celebrate first, then gently suggest next action. Don't immediately push for more referrals.

---

### FAQ

**When should this notification trigger \- signup or activation?**  
 Depends on your definition of success. For quality, wait for activation. For momentum, celebrate at signup but clarify rewards may be pending.

**Should I batch multiple acceptances?**  
 No. Each acceptance deserves individual celebration. Batching reduces emotional impact and makes rewards less clear.

**How do I handle rejected or expired referrals?**  
 Don't notify about rejections. For expired referrals that later convert directly, consider partial recognition to maintain goodwill.

**What if users game the referral system?**  
 Build in verification steps and cooling periods. Celebrate legitimate referrals immediately but delay rewards until verification completes.
