### What this template solves

- Maximizes referral conversion by following up on sent invitations.
- Reduces dropoff from invites lost in email or forgotten by recipients.
- Automates the awkward "did you get my invite?" follow-up process.
- Improves referral program ROI by increasing invite-to-signup conversion.

---

### When to use this template

Use this template when:

- Users can invite others to join your platform via referral links or codes.
- You have a formal referral program with incentives for successful invites.
- Your growth relies on word-of-mouth and user-driven acquisition.
- Invite acceptance rates are lower than desired without follow-up.
- You want to help referrers succeed without requiring manual effort.

---

### How it works (step-by-step)

1. **Trigger.** User sends referral invite to friend's email.
2. **Send initial invite.** Deliver personalized invitation from referrer.
3. **Set reminder timer.** Wait appropriate time (e.g., 3 days).
4. **Send reminder.** If workflow has not been cancelled, send friendly follow-up reminder.

---

### Best practices

- **Personalization drives conversion.** Include referrer's name prominently. "Sarah thinks you'd love \[Product\]" outperforms generic invites.
- **Value proposition clarity.** Explain what referee gets (free trial, discount, exclusive access) within first two sentences.
- **Optimal reminder timing.** 3-5 days balances urgency with avoiding annoyance. Adjust based on your product's consideration cycle.
- **Social proof in reminders.** Include how many mutual connections are already using the product if applicable.

---

### Common mistakes to avoid

- **Sending from no-reply addresses.** Referrals are personal. Let referees reply with questions. Route responses appropriately.
- **Generic referrer attribution.** "A friend invited you" kills trust. Always include specific referrer name and ideally their photo.
- **Aggressive follow-up frequency.** One reminder is sufficient. Multiple reminders from referral invites can damage brand perception.
- **Not handling expired invites.** Include clear expiration terms and what happens after. Allow referrers to resend if needed.

---

### FAQ

**Should reminders come from the referrer or the platform?**  
 Initial invite can appear from referrer, but reminder should clearly come from platform to avoid making referrer seem pushy.

**What's the optimal delay before sending a reminder?**  
 B2B products: 3-5 days. Consumer products: 5-7 days. High-consideration products: 7-14 days. Test and optimize based on your conversion data.

**How do I handle referral invite limits?**  
 Implement reasonable daily/weekly limits to prevent spam. Show remaining invites clearly and reset periodically for engaged users.

**Should I notify referrers when their invites are opened but not accepted?**  
 Generally no. This creates pressure and awkwardness. Only notify on meaningful actions like signup completion.
